About Dr. Lakshmikant Tiwari (TiwariLK)

Academic Background & Affiliations

Lakshmikant Tiwari is currently a Research Fellow (GIS) at the Varghese Kurien Policy Lab, Institute of Rural Management Anand (IRMA). He previously worked as a Research Associate at the Indian Council of Forest Research and Education (ICFRE). He holds an MTech in Remote Sensing and GIS from Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra, and a Master's in Geography from Banaras Hindu University (BHU).

Research Focus & GeoAI Specialization

His core expertise lies at the intersection of GeoAI, causal inference, and smart computing (AI/ML/DL) for earth and environmental systems. His work focuses on:

  • Hydrological modelling and watershed health assessment for sustainable agriculture.
  • Climate change vulnerability and socio-ecological risk assessment using geospatial statistics.
  • Spatio-temporal landscape dynamics and land use/land cover (LULC) change analysis.
  • Application of Google Earth Engine (GEE), Python, and R programming for big data analytics in natural resource management (NRM).
  • Microwave remote sensing and Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) for soil moisture estimation and disaster risk modelling.

Key Projects (e.g., REWARD, Sone Beel)

He is the lead geospatial researcher for the REWARD (Rejuvenating Watershed for Agricultural Resilience through Innovative Development) project in Karnataka. He also serves as Co-PI for the Sone Beel wetland management study funded by Assam University, focusing on hydro-climatic dynamics.
